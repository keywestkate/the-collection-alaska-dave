Activities

Where the Road Ends

Homer is the end of the Alaska road system — and the beginning of everything else. Kachemak Bay State Park, art galleries, whale watching, and flyouts to remote Alaska.

Marine Wildlife
Whale Watching

Humpback, Orca, and Beluga whale excursions on Kachemak Bay. Guided boat tours departing from the Homer Spit — arranged through the Hub 3 concierge.

Wilderness Park
Kachemak Bay State Park

400,000+ acres of wilderness accessible only by boat or floatplane. Kayaking, hiking, tidepool exploration, and remote camping. Water taxi from the Spit.

Aerial
Glacier Flyouts

Bear viewing at Katmai, Harding Icefield overflight, remote fly-in fishing at private lakes. Floatplane departures from Homer Airport coordinated through the collection.

Culture
Art & Galleries

Homer has one of Alaska's most vibrant art communities. Pratt Museum, Pioneer Avenue galleries, and local artist studios — an afternoon walk off the Spit.

Water
Sea Kayaking

Guided sea kayaking tours through Kachemak Bay — glacier fronts, sea otter habitat, and remote coves inaccessible by any other means. All skill levels welcome.

Events
Ocean-View Celebrations

Intimate ceremonies and elopements with Kachemak Bay as the backdrop. Baycrest Lodge and the Jellyfish unit host private events by arrangement — contact the concierge.

Dining

Homer's Table

Fresh halibut from the morning's catch, Spit waterfront dining, and farm-to-table Alaska cuisine. Homer punches far above its weight on the food scene.

On-Property
Private Chef

Your halibut and salmon prepared that evening at Baycrest Lodge or the Jellyfish unit. Whole fish presentations, sashimi boards, and Alaskan seafood courses on request.

Request Chef

Wellness & Recovery

Spa

Coastal

Views
