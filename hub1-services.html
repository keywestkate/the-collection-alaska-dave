Cooper Landing, Alaska

What We
Offer.

Guided fishing, wilderness activities, celebrations, and curated Alaska experiences — all rooted in the Upper Kenai River corridor.

Core Service

Guided Fishing

Cooper Landing Fishing Guide, LLC has been guiding on the Kenai and Kasilof Rivers for over 20 years. Every trip is customized — species, technique, duration, and group size.

May – July
King Salmon

The King of all Alaska salmon. Trophy Chinook on the Kenai River — drift boat, gear and fly fishing. Full day and private boat options. Up to 4 anglers per boat.

View Rates
June – August
Sockeye Salmon

The most abundant run on the Kenai — up to 300,000 fish per day at peak. Bank and drift fishing with fly and conventional gear. A bucket-list experience for every angler.

View Rates
August – October
Silver Salmon

Aggressive fighters in the 10–20 lb range. Silver (Coho) salmon on the Kenai are a fly angler's dream — great on streamers, excellent for all skill levels.

View Rates
May – October
Rainbow Trout

World-class rainbow trout on the Upper Kenai River. These are big fish — 20"+ specimens are common. Dave's signature trout spey technique is world-renowned in fly fishing circles.

View Rates
Year-Round
Dolly Varden

Dolly Varden char run the Kenai throughout the season, often in company with salmon. A hard-fighting, beautifully colored fish — excellent on streamer flies and great for beginners.

View Rates
August – October
Steelhead

Alaska steelhead are rare — and that rarity makes every hookup extraordinary. Private boat only, 1–2 anglers. Dave's most specialized and sought-after trip.

View Rates
Specialty

Fly Fishing & Trout Spey

Dave Lisi is a published fly fishing contributor and trout spey specialist. His signature technique — trout spey on the Upper Kenai — has been featured in national fly fishing publications and on YouTube. The Cooper Landing Fly Shop carries his custom patterns.

Guided walk-in fishing at the Russian River, Quartz Creek, and Anchor River. No boat required — ideal for guests who prefer shore fishing or who want a different experience from the drift boat.

Beyond Fishing

Activities at Hub 1

Cooper Landing sits at the gateway to the Kenai Peninsula. When the rods are put away, the adventure continues.

River
Kenai River Rafting

Scenic float tours and Class V rapid experiences at Six-Mile Creek. From family-friendly drift to white-knuckle whitewater — arranged through the lodge concierge.

Equestrian
Horseback Riding

Guided trail rides through the hills above Cooper Landing. Arranged through the lodge — all skill levels accommodated, from first-time riders to experienced equestrians.

Trails
300+ Miles of Hiking

The Kenai Peninsula offers over 300 miles of maintained trails. Riverside walks, ridge hikes, and high-elevation routes with panoramic views of the Chugach and Kenai Mountains.

Water
Kayaking

Afternoon paddling on Kenai Lake with local guides, or sea kayak expeditions in Resurrection Bay near Seward. Guided trips arranged through the concierge.

Historic
Gold Panning

Search for Alaska gold with local panning services. A classic Alaska experience — great for families, and genuinely productive in the Cooper Landing area streams.

Marine
Whale Watching

Guided day trips to witness Alaska's marine life — humpback and orca whale watching in Kenai Fjords National Park, departing from Seward. A 2-hour drive from Hub 1.

Celebrations

Weddings & Events

Alaska Heavenly Lodge hosts ceremonies, receptions, and private events on its 12,000 sq ft of manicured riverfront lawns. Up to 25 overnight guests, 50 for events. Ceremony arbor, round tables, linens, and full kitchen included.
