Fishing — Homer

Pacific Halibut & Saltwater Fishing

Homer is Alaska's halibut capital. The Kachemak Bay and outer Gulf waters hold some of the largest Pacific halibut in the world, with fish regularly topping 100–200 lbs.

The Method

How We Fish Kachemak Bay

01
Deep Anchoring

Anchor on the deep mudflats of Kachemak Bay and lower chunks of bait — halibut lie flat on the bottom and come to the boat when conditions are right.

02
Drift Fishing

Slow drifts over halibut-holding structure with circle hooks and herring. Effective in lighter winds, covers more ground, and tends to produce multiple fish in good conditions.

03
Trolling for Silver

Flashers and hoochies trolled near the surface at moderate speed to intercept school silver salmon staging in the bay. Spinning and conventional gear provided.

04
Jigging

Vertical jigging with heavy iron or lead-headed lures is the most physically exciting method — and the best way to target lingcod and rockfish alongside halibut on the same trip.

Beyond the Bay

Flyout Fishing
& Katmai Bear Viewing

Homer's greatest geographic advantage is its air-service hub. Float planes and bush planes connect you to remote river drainages, wilderness lodges, and the Alaska Peninsula in under 90 minutes.

Katmai National Park — home to the world's largest brown bear population — is accessible via floatplane from Homer. Watch bears fish sockeye at Brooks Falls or forage the coastal flats of Hallo Bay.

Remote river fly fishing trips target wild rainbow trout, Dolly Varden, and sockeye on Alaska Peninsula drainages that see minimal pressure. A full day out and back, guided by specialists who know these rivers.

Homer Experiences

Beyond Fishing

Homer has more depth than almost any other small town in Alaska. The Spit, the arts community, the coast, and the park make it a destination in its own right.

Photo: whale-watching-kachemak-bay.jpg
Ocean Tour

Whale Watching

Humpback and Orca whales frequent Kachemak Bay throughout the summer. Guided boat tours from Homer Harbor put you in prime viewing territory alongside sea otters, Dall's porpoise, and abundant seabirds.

Photo: kachemak-bay-state-park.jpg
State Park

Kachemak Bay State Park

Accessible only by boat or plane — no roads — Kachemak Bay State Park is one of Alaska's most spectacular undeveloped coastlines. Hiking, sea kayaking, tidepool exploring, and backcountry camping.

Photo: sea-kayaking-homer-spit.jpg
Paddle

Sea Kayaking

Guided sea kayak tours through the protected inner waters of Kachemak Bay. Paddle alongside sea otters, explore kelp forests, and access sea caves and coastal cliffs not reachable by motor boat.

Photo: homer-spit-art-galleries.jpg
Culture

Art Galleries & the Spit

Homer's Pratt Museum and the galleries along the Spit represent Alaska's most vibrant arts community outside of Anchorage. Mix world-class nature with genuine Alaska culture on a day in town.

Photo: homer-coastal-hiking.jpg
Hiking

Coastal Trails

The bluffs above Homer offer trail systems with panoramic views of Kachemak Bay and the Kenai Mountains. Baycrest and Skyline Drive trails are accessible from the lodge — no car needed.

Coastal Wellness

Reset at the
Edge of Everything.

The ocean does something that rivers can't. The scale of Kachemak Bay, the tidal rhythms, the smell of salt air — guests consistently describe their Homer stays as the most restorative part of the Alaska Circuit.

Baycrest Lodge's panoramic bay views, the private perch of the Ocean Bluff property, and the historic warmth of the Driftwood Inn each offer a different kind of coastal rest.

No specific recovery program — just uncommonly beautiful space, good food, and the kind of quiet that only comes when the nearest city is 225 miles of highway away.
