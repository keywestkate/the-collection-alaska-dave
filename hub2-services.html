What We
Offer at Hub 2.

Kasilof and Lower Kenai River guided fishing, wilderness activities, the Keen Eye recovery program, and direct-book cabin lodging in the heart of the Kenai Peninsula.

Core Service

Guided Fishing

The Kasilof Salmon Guide service — same team as Cooper Landing Fishing Guide, LLC — operates 20-foot drift boats on the Kasilof River and Lower Kenai. Back trolling, bobber fishing, spin fishing, and fly fishing for King, Sockeye, Silver salmon and Steelhead.

Kasilof River · Mid-May – July
King Salmon

The Kasilof has a distinct, separate King salmon run from the Kenai — a wilder, less pressured fishery. Trophy Chinook in the 50–80 lb class. Back trolling with plugs and spinners from 20-foot drift boats.

Kasilof & Lower Kenai · June – August
Sockeye Salmon

The Lower Kenai and Kasilof both carry massive sockeye runs through late June and July. Bank fishing and drift techniques. The most productive red salmon fishing on the peninsula.

View Rates
August – October
Silver Salmon

Silver (Coho) salmon run the Kasilof from early August through October. Aggressive, hard-fighting fish in the 10–15 lb range. Excellent on both conventional and fly tackle.

View Rates
August – October
Steelhead

The Kasilof River carries a fall steelhead run — one of Alaska's least-pressured and most coveted fisheries. Private boat only, 1–2 anglers. Contact for availability and conditions.

Technique

Four Ways to Fish the Kasilof

The Kasilof fishes differently from the Kenai. The guide team at Hub 2 uses four primary techniques depending on species, conditions, and guest preference — and they teach every technique on the water.

01
Back Trolling
Plugs and spinners rowed upstream — primary King salmon technique
02
Bobber Fishing
Float presentation with bait or flies — dominant sockeye method
03
Spin Fishing
Casting lures and spinners — versatile across all species
04
Fly Fishing
Streamer and dry fly — excellent for Silver salmon and steelhead
